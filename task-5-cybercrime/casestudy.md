## Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sending money through fake payment requests or scams. This type of fraud has increased with the widespread use of digital payment apps.

Typically, the fraud begins when the victim receives a phone call, message, or link from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer/seller. The attacker may ask the victim to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request” under the pretext of receiving money. In reality, approving such requests results in money being deducted from the victim’s account.

College students and online sellers are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps and may not be fully aware of such scams. The fraudsters exploit urgency and confusion to manipulate victims into making quick decisions.

The consequences include financial loss, compromised personal information, and emotional stress. In some cases, victims may also lose access to their accounts if further details are shared.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and cautious behavior while using digital payment platforms.
